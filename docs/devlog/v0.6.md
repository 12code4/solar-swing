# v0.6 - the system moves

The planets orbit now. That sounds like a small change. It rewrote how the whole game feels.

Up to v0.5 the solar system was a diorama. Nine planets nailed in place, and you flew between them. Now every planet sweeps around the sun on a real circular orbit, speed set by the actual gravity at its radius. Earth ends up doing about 50 metres per second. Mercury does 81. Pluto crawls along at 8.

Here is the thing I did not see coming. Once the ground under you is moving at 50 m/s, "standing still" stops being a thing. Every walk, jump and landing rule had to be rewritten to run in the frame of whatever body you are standing on. If you skip that, walking east on Earth means walking at 59 m/s and west means 41, and the planet just slides out from under your feet.

Landing changed the most. You cannot point at Mars and burn any more. Mars is not where you are aiming, it is where it will be in ninety seconds. And when you get there you have to match its velocity or you skid off the surface at 41 m/s. I tested this. Arriving matched, you plant and stay put. Arriving unmatched, you slide 63 units and lose the planet. That is a rendezvous, and it was an accident of the physics rather than something I designed.

Two real bugs fell out of it. Atmospheric drag was braking against the sun instead of against the planet, so standing in Earth's atmosphere applied about 30 m/s2 of sideways brake and you could never land. And my own spec double counted the orbital motion, which moved you at exactly twice the planet's speed.

The slingshots work properly now. Same approach past Jupiter, moving planet versus frozen planet, and you come out 51 m/s different. That is a real gravity assist.

Also in: NASA textures with the procedural terrain still painting over them, 120 asteroids instead of 34, a log scale minimap, and free look on ALT.

Jupiter is still a monster. Escape velocity at its cloud tops is 398 m/s and you fly at 40. Nothing to be done about that this version.
