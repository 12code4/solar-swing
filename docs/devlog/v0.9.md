# v0.9 - seeded skies

Two changes this time, and they both came straight from playing the game and getting annoyed.

First, the solar system was always the same. Same Earth in the same spot, same route to Mars, forever. Now every load rolls a seed and every planet starts somewhere new on its orbit. The asteroid belt swings around as a single wedge too. The layout you get is the run you play. If you find a spicy one, the seed is right there on the start screen and ?seed=424242 in the URL brings it back. Sharing a layout costs nine digits.

I was careful about what the seed does NOT touch. Terrain, craters, rock shapes and sizes are all still fixed. Only the orbital phase shuffles. That keeps the world recognizable, and it keeps the asteroid count deterministic so the physics budget never surprises me.

Second, the touch controls. The old scheme burned in whatever direction you pushed the right stick, which meant your thumb was doing vector math while the camera did something else. Gone. Now there is one rule: the camera is your nozzle. Aim, then throttle. The throttle is a vertical slider on the right edge, and it stays where you set it, like cruise control. Heat still drains by throttle, overheating still shuts you down.

To make aim-is-thrust honest, the camera needed real freedom. The pitch clamp went from about 72 degrees to about 89, so you can look and burn nearly straight up or down. That broke the camera at the poles, because lookAt was getting an up hint almost parallel to the view and rolling wildly. The fix is to hand it the exact perpendicular instead, built from the right axis. Same camera at shallow angles, stable at steep ones. The fragile up-blend basis I did not touch.

One number for honesty: full throttle pointed straight up on Earth still does not lift you. Thrust 38, surface gravity 44.8. Jump first, burn sideways. That rule is the game, and the slider does not get to repeal it.

The salvage-run branch from earlier today is parked, not dead. It needs a rebase onto this before it comes back.
