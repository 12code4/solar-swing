# v0.10 - full circle

This one started as a bug report from my own playtest: at high speed the camera falls so far behind that looking around stops working. The cause was embarrassing once I wrote it down. The camera chased its ideal spot with a lerp in world space, and against a moving target that always leaves a gap proportional to speed. At 400 m/s the gap is about 52 units. The camera sits 8 units away normally. So the whole game was happening in front of a camera parked way outside it. The fix: smooth the camera's offset from the player instead of its world position. Same feel standing still, zero lag at any speed. Four lines.

The brake is the change I think people will feel most. Space is slippery, and until now the only way to shed a bad direction was to flip around and eyeball a retro-burn. Now you hold B, or the BRAKE button on a phone, and the jetpack auto-aims against your velocity relative to the nearest body. Same engine, same 38 thrust, same heat bar, and overheating still shuts it off. It cannot add energy, only remove it, so orbital mechanics stays honest. It just removes the aiming chore from the one burn that was pure chore. The last step scales itself so you stop exactly, no jitter.

The asteroid belt finally encircles the sun. It had been a 35 degree wedge since v0.2, which made sense when the whole system spanned one screen. Now it is a full ring, sparse on purpose, still 120 rocks, with the three dense knots landing at seeded angles each run.

And the planets got some life. Earth wears a rotating cloud deck pulled from the same CC-BY texture set, and it only appears if the texture actually loads, same as every map. Gas giant cloud shells now spin slowly, so Jupiter's spot drifts. All of it is strictly visual. Rocky planets still hold still, because spinning them means spinning the collision field and dragging the player along with the surface, and that is a real physics job, not a decoration. It is in the backlog with the warning written on it.
